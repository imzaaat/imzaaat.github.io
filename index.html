Hey There!

My name is Zaid Abu-Tarboush. I don’t know how to introduce myself, rather how to classify it... I’m a third-year electrical engineering student at The Hashemite University. But I’m all about computer science and its applications. My passion started when I was five years old. When I broke my first computer trying to learn how it functions. Ever since, I’ve been a tireless seeker of knowledge of that field.

I believe that engineering isn’t just a bunch of courses you take. Engineering, to me, is a way of thinking, it’s a mindset. Engineering is being able to learn what you need to, to get the job done. That’s when I started to believe that I can be a robust innovative person in the technology industry without having a degree. Because I have the most important skill of all time, the ability to learn.

I expect being an entrepreneur anytime. Being a polymath (it’s a bliss, not a curse) with many interests gives me the ability to have breadth knowledge and deep thoughts from different backgrounds to apply to whatever I’m working on. Another positive side, having an idea what a person does and feels in a specific position makes working with teams easy for me. Because I understand them better.
I’m currently working to shape out my career, seemingly in artificial intelligence. Probably merge that with space exploration.

When I’m not doing anything related to science, you can find me reading, drawing, playing video games, listening to music, or taking a walk.
