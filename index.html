Assembly Tic-Tac-Toc

Like any other tic-tac-toc game, the only difference it was developed by x86 assembly with the help of various interrupts such as, 10h and 21h.

This very project has been an outstanding experience for me. It gave me an idea about how hard (and fun, maybe?) it was to develop computer software in the early days of computers. Writing programs with assembly makes you feel overpowered, it gives you infinite amount of power to do whatever you want, literally!

Aside of the hard debugging process with assembly, I think assembly is my new favorite programming language. I feel like I understand how is everything built, working, and evolving. And on top of that, I can read The Art Of Computer Programming by Donald Knuth, the greatest book on algorithms ever, which examples are written in assembly.

I have made a video to preview this "game." You can find it here.

This Website

That's where you are right now! In a world where a toaster or coffee maker can connect to the internet (Internet of Things has progressed), one needs to be present online to connect with others. Having a website has advantages no other social media site has.

Having little knowledge about web design, this project was a great opportunity to learn. I learned so much the hard way. Probably the lesson that I will never forget is; Design for mobiles first, then other devices. I learned this after I had to rewrite the site from scratch. because it was working on PCs, but not mobiles.

I could've used content management systems like WordPress or Joomla. But building the site from scratch alone, with my bare hands makes it more valuable for me. This also motivates me to keep it updated. I don't want it to be a random site on the internet which was last updated in 2007.

Currently, the site is operating on Version 2. You can check out Version 1 here.
